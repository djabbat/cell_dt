From Centriole to Senescence

CDATA proposes a single molecular cascade: reactive oxygen species irreversibly detach inductor complexes from centrioles, reducing stem cell potency and driving every hallmark of aging downstream.

Aging Tracks

Five Parallel Pathways

Centriolar damage propagates through five mechanistically distinct tracks. Each track is modeled as a separate Rust module with its own ECS component, feeding back into the central damage state.

TRACK A
Ciliary / Morphogen Failure
CEP164↓ → cilia↓ → Shh/Wnt↓
→ regeneration_tempo↓
Primary cilia are the antennae of stem cells. Loss of CEP164 integrity collapses ciliation, shutting down morphogen reception and niche self-renewal.
TRACK B
Spindle / Pool Exhaustion
spindle_fidelity↓ → symmetric div↑
→ stem cell pool↓
Spindle misassembly forces symmetric differentiating divisions, depleting the HSC niche pool. The asymmetric division module tracks this depletion dynamically.
TRACK C
Telomere Attrition
shortening = per_div × div_rate
× spindle_f × ros_f → Hayflick G1
Each mitotic division erodes telomeres proportional to spindle fidelity and ROS level. Critical shortening triggers permanent G1 arrest via p21 and the Hayflick limit.
TRACK D
Epigenetic Clock
methylation_age += dt
× (1 + damage × 0.5)
Oxidative damage accelerates epigenetic drift. Division-coupled methylation inheritance propagates the clock state to daughter cells, modeling Horvath clock acceleration.
TRACK E
Mitochondrial Dysfunction
mtDNA mutations → ETC↓ → ROS↑
→ mito_shield↓ → O₂ at centrosome↑
Mitochondrial ROS production, fusion/fission balance, and mitophagy flux are modeled in the dedicated MitochondrialModule. The mito-shield is the central O₂ barrier mechanism.
Inductor System

Molecular Basis of Potency

Each centriole carries its own set of inductor complexes — distinct molecules on mother (M) and daughter (D) centrioles. O₂ access is the sole irreversible trigger of detachment. Potency is a function of both counts.

The CDATA Hypothesis

Jaba Tkemaladze proposed that the universal nature of aging across eukaryotes can be explained by a single convergent mechanism: progressive oxidative modification of inductor complexes attached to centrioles. Unlike telomere theory (which requires cell division) or mitochondrial theory (which is a downstream effect), CDATA places the centriole at the causal origin.

Why Centrioles?

Centrioles are the only non-membrane-enclosed organelles that persist across cell generations without complete turnover. The mother centriole can be decades old in post-mitotic neurons. This persistence makes them uniquely vulnerable to cumulative oxidative damage — and uniquely positioned to encode developmental history through inductor count.
